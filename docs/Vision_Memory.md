## Category & positioning (canonical)

digiStiel is an **AI-Powered Conversational Value Creation Platform**.
Tagline: **From Goals to Measurable Value.**

It is building a **new category**: a platform that helps organizations
continuously turn business goals into measurable value — combining the
scalability of software, the impact of consulting, and the leverage of AI.

Explicitly **not**: an AI tool, a CRM, a consultancy, a software vendor, or an
agency. It does not sell time (≠ consultant) and does not sell functionality
(≠ software). It is measured by the value customers realize.

Operational tone (from the customer-facing work): **operational clarity for
local business owners**, without consultant-speak, AI-hype or startup jargon.
Founder lens: Six Sigma / Lean manufacturing. The target user's ideal reaction:
*"Amai, eindelijk iemand die begrijpt waar wij vastlopen."*

## Long-term ambition (canonical)

digiStiel is designed as a **European** AI-Powered Conversational Value Creation
Platform. Belgium and the Netherlands are **validation and scaling markets**,
not end markets. Long-term ambition is European expansion via a repeatable
value-creation model. (Europe chosen over Benelux (too small) and worldwide
(too complex): large enough, culturally manageable, relatively homogeneous
regulation, huge SMB market, credible to investors.)

## Capital philosophy (canonical) — "Capital Follows Value"

Capital does not create value; capital accelerates value creation. The company
must be able to prove value **without** investment. Capital is used to
accelerate customer acquisition, partnerships, hiring and expansion — not to
prove the business exists. Strongest investor narrative: "We can grow without
capital", not "We need capital to survive."

## Brand architecture — Aevor House of Brands (canonical; see `BrandArchitecture.md`)

The company is an international **AI Platform Company**, a House of Brands.
Layers: **Aevor** (corporate/holding, aevor.eu registered) → **Platform
Portfolio** (holds the engines; owns cross-engine standards/governance/Capability
Contract/compatibility, no services) → **Atlas** (the *first* Platform Engine, not
"the" platform; internal name, versioned; external only as "Powered by Atlas")
with five internal capabilities (Bifrost, Aegis, Argus, Heimdall, Yggdrasil —
Atlas' own, engine-bound, never external) → Commercial Brands + white-label route
→ **Value Plan** (central commercial object) → **Commercial Engagement Models**
(Start/Flow/Boost) → AI Agents.

**digiStiel is a Commercial Brand** — Belgium + Netherlands, **1–50 FTE only**,
**Revenue Operations value creation only** — the first brand under Atlas/Aevor,
not the company. Three name kinds: external brands (Aevor, digiStiel — full
naming framework + domain), the Atlas endorsement brand (light check, no own
domain), and purely-internal capability names (no check, never external).

Five Vision principles (full text in `BrandArchitecture.md`): corporate inspires
trust / commercial communicates value; platform is independent of commercial
brands; commercial brands = markets not feature bundles; **brand architecture
follows platform architecture, never the reverse**; architecture must support
20+ years of international growth and future acquisitions.

## Strategic focus

Revenue Operations is the **beachhead, not the destination** — most measurable,
strongest founder expertise, fastest route to proof of value. Long-term vision
is broader; expansions stay out of scope for Business Case V1. (Under the House
of Brands model, broader value domains and larger segments become *future
commercial brands* on the same platform engine — not an expansion of the
digiStiel brand itself.)

## Long-term direction (the deeper opportunity)

Evolution: **value realization → continuous learning → predictive value
creation.** A platform that becomes more valuable as more value is created
(not as more features are added). Ultimately helps organizations anticipate and
predict where value can be created next — not by replacing human judgment, but
by improving decision quality from accumulated organizational learning.

## Two explicit Vision corrections made to the Business Case
