## The ten core beliefs (protected — change only with strong evidence)

1. **Businesses don't buy AI, they buy outcomes** (the most important belief).
2. **Value comes before technology** (protection against feature creep).
3. **Conversation is the most natural UI** — "software should learn the user".
4. **Discovery creates more value than immediate answers** — understand first,
   advise second; discovery may itself be a commercial asset.
5. **Customers should experience value before commitment** — internal "Value
   First": Conversation → Discovery → Opportunity → Value → Commitment.
6. **Value creation is continuous** — recurring revenue from recurring value,
   not contractual lock-in.
7. **Best growth comes from existing customers.**
8. **Capital should accelerate value, not create it** ("Capital Follows Value").
9. **Revenue Operations is the beachhead, not the destination.**
10. **Value creation is more important than software.**

Three beliefs to actively test: do customers truly buy value not software? does
conversational discovery scale economically? does recurring value creation beat
SaaS retention/expansion?

## Origin story

Started not from an AI breakthrough but from one real customer experimenting
with AI who (1) didn't know how to create value and (2) was dependent on an
agency (~€600/month). Core problem = lack of structured value discovery, not
lack of AI.

## Business-model evolution (8 stages → current thinking)

AI Agent → Guidance → Value Creation → Value Plan → Discovery breakthrough →
Start/Flow/Boost → Advice breakthrough → Revenue Share. Every move toward value
made the business stronger; every move toward AI/features made it weaker.

**Commercial layers (NOT products — decided). Now formally = Commercial
Engagement Models:** Start/Flow/Boost define *how* a Value Plan is delivered
(contract form, pricing, invoicing, service level, delivery approach, intensity)
— never *what* the customer buys. They appear **after** a Value Plan exists, and
are platform/brand-independent (reusable across commercial brands). Pricing,
contracts and recurring revenue attach to Engagement Models, not to products.
- **digiStart** (Activate/Land) — prove first measurable value, low barrier.
- **digiFlow** (Recur) — continuous optimisation; expected largest, most
  predictable revenue component.
- **digiBoost** (Expand) — additional initiatives within existing relationships.

**Revenue architecture (5 layers):** L1 Recurring platform (subscription) ·
L2 Advice consumption (wallet) · L3 Expansion (Boost) · L4 Selective
outcome-based · L5 Limited services. Outcome-based stays **supplementary**, not
primary (unpredictable cashflow, disputes, investor discomfort).

## Key concepts

- **Advice Wallet** (strongest monetisation hypothesis): customers pay for
  ongoing access to opportunities, not "advice #147". Model = subscription +
  advice wallet + expansion revenue. Advice = economic object / proxy for value.
- **Maturity-based payment** (decided): not prepaid-vs-success, but **both**,
  depending on relationship maturity. Phase 1 (building trust, no track record):
  MRR + success fee + revenue share, pay on realised value. Phase 2 (trust
  built): prepaid budget / wallet / annual value contracts become logical.
  Analogy: consultants are paid upfront on trust, not on already-realised value.
- **Land & Expand:** organizations never run out of value opportunities; the
  relationship is cumulative, not project-based. NRR is "the core bet". First
  sale most expensive; expansion = lower sales cost, faster decisions, higher
  conversion.

## Pricing model (the live xlsx — placeholders, not booked revenue)

Hybrid cost-floor + value-ceiling. **Important warning:** in the *prototype*
scenario (15 customers) the value ceiling (€3,600/yr) is **below** the cost
floor (€9,028/yr) → "CEILING < FLOOR", i.e. not profitably priceable at that
volume. Only the *scaled* scenario (250 customers) is VIABLE (~75% margin,
LTV:CAC ~33x). The two numbers that move valuation most: **gross margin** and
**LTV:CAC**, both driven by **human effort per customer** and **reuse rate**
(model sections D and F). Replace placeholders with real Platform + Sales data
before any raise.
